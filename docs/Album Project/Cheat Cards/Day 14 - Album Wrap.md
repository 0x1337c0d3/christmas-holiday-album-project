## Production Setup

### Hardware Synths:
- Sequential Prophet 12 — pads, chords, drones
- Nord Lead 2X — leads, motifs, arpeggios
- Roland VSynth XT — textures, time-stretch, formants
- Yamaha FS1R — primary Side B formant pads
- Moog Little Phatty — all bass parts
- Dave Smith Evolver — textures, weird modulation
- Korg Volca Beats — percussion elements (sampled)

### Organic Instruments:
- Fender Telecaster — clean arpeggios, volume swells
- Upright Piano — felted/muted, sparse notes

### DAW & Signal Chain:
- Ableton Live 11
- Synths → Apogee Ensemble → ADAT → RME BabyFace
- Drums: VSynth XT + Volca sampled into Drum Rack

### Processing:
- Side A drums: Tape saturation, short plate reverb (0.3-0.5s), gentle compression
- Side A synths: Bus reverb (short plate), minimal processing
- Side B: Long hall reverb (12-40s), no compression, minimal EQ

---

## Cohesion Elements

1. **Melodic:** Track 1 motif recurs in Track 5 and Movement III
2. **Textural:** Evolver patch appears in Tracks 1, 3, 4, and Movement II
3. **Sonic:** FS1R hinted in Track 5, central to all Side B movements
4. **Harmonic:** Tonal arc from A → C → D → E → C# → E → B → C

---

## What Worked

- 1-hour creative sprints kept momentum high
- Fixed sound palette eliminated decision fatigue
- Sampling drums once saved hours across all tracks
- Alternating creative approaches (harmony-first vs rhythm-first) kept things fresh
- Side B's patient approach created necessary contrast

## Task 7: Celebrate & Step Away (Rest of Day)

### Seriously: Stop Working

You've just:
- Composed 9-11 pieces of music
- Created ~40 minutes of original material
- Executed a disciplined creative sprint
- Completed a conceptual album in 14 days

**This is a significant achievement.**

### What to Do Now:

1. **Listen to the album once more** (as a listener, not a creator)
2. **Share with trusted friend/collaborator** (optional, but feedback can be valuable)
3. **Write a quick journal entry** about the experience
4. **Put the project away for at least 2-4 weeks**

### Why Step Away:

- Your ears are fatigued
- You're too close to the material to be objective
- The urge to "fix" things is strongest now (resist it)
- Distance will reveal what actually matters

**When you return in January/February:**
- You'll hear it with fresh ears
- Obvious issues will be obvious
- Strong elements will still be strong
- You can decide: Leave as-is OR polish further
